<b>Background:</b><i>CYP2C9</i> and <i>VKORC1</i> are important factors in warfarin metabolism. The authors explored the effects of these genetic polymorphisms and clinical factors on a warfarin maintenance dose and then established the prediction algorithm for Honghe minorities in China. <b>Materials & methods:</b> Quantitative fluorescence PCR determined the mutation frequency of <i>CYP2C9</i> and <i>VKORC1-1639</i> G>A alleles. The authors collected the relevant clinical factors, including age, gender, body surface area (BSA), international normalized ratio value, daily warfarin dose, comorbidity and concomitant prescriptions. <b>Results:</b> The mean values of BSA and international normalized ratio in Honghe minorities were lower than in Han Chinese (p = 0.00). The genotype of <i>CYP2C9*1/*1</i> and <i>VKORC1</i>-<i>1639</i> AA was the main allele, the mutationfrequency of <i>VKORC1-1639</i> AA and the number of male of Honghe minorities were lower than that of Han Chinese (p = 0.013 and p = 0.04). The significances of the effect on actual warfarin dose value were gender, <i>VKORC1</i> AA mutant, <i>CYP2C9*1/*1</i>, age, hypertension and BSA sequentially. <b>Conclusion:</b> By multiple linear regression analysis with genetic and clinical factors, the authors determined a prediction algorithm for adjusting individual dosing of warfarin in this population. Clinical trial registration number: ChiCTR2100051778.
