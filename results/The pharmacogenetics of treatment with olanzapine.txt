Genetic polymorphism in olanzapine-metabolizing enzymes, transporters and drug targets is associated with alterations in safety and efficacy. The aim of this systematic review is to describe all clinically relevant pharmacogenetic information on olanzapine and to propose clinically actionable variants. Two hundred and eighty-four studies were screened; 76 complied with the inclusion criteria and presented significant associations. <i>DRD2</i> Taq1A (rs1800497) *A1, <i>LEP</i> -2548 (rs7799039) G and <i>CYP1A2</i>*1F alleles were related to olanzapine effectiveness and safety variability in several studies, with a high level of evidence. <i>DRD2</i> -141 (rs1799732) Ins, A-241G (rs1799978) G, <i>DRD3</i> Ser9Gly (rs6280) Gly, <i>HTR2A</i> rs7997012 A, <i>ABCB1</i> C3435T (rs1045642) T and G2677T/A (rs2032582) T and <i>UGT1A4</i>*3 alleles were related to safety, effectiveness and/or pharmacokinetic variability with moderated level of evidence.
