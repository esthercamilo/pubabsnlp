Gastric cancer (GC) is one of the major causes of cancer-related mortality worldwide. The vast majority of GC cases are adenocarcinomas including intestinal and diffuse GC. The incidence of diffuse GCs, often associated with poor overall survival, has constantly increased in USA and Europe The molecular basis of diffuse GC aggressivity remains unclear. Using mRNA from diffuse and intestinal GC tumor samples of a Western cohort, this study reports the expression level of the immunomodulatory aryl-hydrocarbon receptor (AhR), and genes involved in immune suppression (<i>PD1</i>, <i>PD-L1</i>, <i>PD-L2</i>) and the early steps of tryptophan metabolism (<i>IDO1</i>, <i>IDO2</i>, <i>TDO2</i>). Strongly increased expression of <i>IDO1</i> (<i>p</i> < 0.001) and <i>PD1</i> (<i>p <</i> 0.003) was observed in the intestinal sub-type. The highest expression of <i>IDO1</i> and <i>PDL1</i> correlated with early clinical stage and absence of lymphatic invasion (×25 <i>p</i> = 0.004, ×3 <i>p =</i> 0.04, respectively). Our results suggest that kynurenine, produced by tryptophan catabolism, and AhR activation play a central role in creating an immunosuppressive environment. Correspondingly, as compared to intestinal GCs, expression levels of <i>IDO1-TDO2</i> and <i>PD-L1</i> were less prominent in diffuse GCs which also had less infiltration of immune cells, suggesting an inactive immune response in the advanced diffuse GC. Confirmation of these patterns of gene expression will require a larger cohort of early and advanced stages of diffuse GC samples.
