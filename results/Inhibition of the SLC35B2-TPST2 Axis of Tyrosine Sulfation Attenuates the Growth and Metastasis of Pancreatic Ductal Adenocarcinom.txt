Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) is the fourth leading cause of cancer deaths in the United States. Tyrosine sulfation, catalyzed by the tyrosylprotein sulfotransferase 2 (TPST2), is a post-translational modification essential for protein-protein interactions and cellular functions. Solute carrier family 35 member B (SLC35B2) is a key transporter that transports the universal sulfate donor 3'-phosphoadenosine 5'-phosphosulfate into the Golgi apparatus where the protein sulfation occurs. The goal of this study was to determine whether and how the SLC35B2-TPST2 axis of tyrosine sulfation plays a role in PDAC.
