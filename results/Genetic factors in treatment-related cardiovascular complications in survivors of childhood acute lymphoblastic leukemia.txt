<b>Aim:</b> Cardiovascular disease represents one of the main causes of secondary morbidity and mortality in patients with childhood cancer. <b>Patients & methods:</b> To further address this issue, we analyzed cardiovascular complications in relation to common and rare genetic variants derived through whole-exome sequencing from childhood acute lymphoblastic leukemia survivors (PETALE cohort). <b>Results:</b> Significant associations were detected among common variants in the <i>TTN</i> gene, left ventricular ejection fraction (p ≤ 0.0005), and fractional shortening (p ≤ 0.001). Rare variants enrichment in the <i>NOS1</i>, <i>ABCG2</i> and <i>NOD2</i> was observed in relation to left ventricular ejection fraction, and in <i>NOD2</i> and <i>ZNF267</i> genes in relation to fractional shortening. Following stratification according to risk groups, the modulatory effect of rare variants was additionally found in the <i>CBR1</i>, <i>ABCC5</i> and <i>AKR1C3</i> genes. None of the associations was replicated in St-Jude Lifetime Cohort Study. <b>Conclusion:</b> Further studies are needed to confirm whether the described genetic markers may be useful in identifying patients at increased risk of these complications.
