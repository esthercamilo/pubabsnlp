<b>Aim:</b> To determine if interventional cardiologists' knowledge and attitudes toward pharmacogenetic (PGx) testing influenced their antiplatelet prescribing decisions in response to <i>CYP2C19</i> results. <b>Materials & methods:</b> Surveys were administered prior to participating in a randomized trial of <i>CYP2C19</i> testing. Associations between baseline knowledge/attitudes and agreement with the genotype-guided antiplatelet recommendations were determined using multivariable logistic regression. <b>Results:</b> 50% believed that PGx testing would be valuable to predict medication toxicity or efficacy. 64% felt well informed about PGx testing and its therapeutic application. However, PGx experience, knowledge, nor attitudes were significantly associated with agreement to genotype-guided antiplatelet recommendations. <b>Conclusion:</b> Cardiologists' knowledge and attitudes were not associated with <i>CYP2C19-</i>guided antiplatelet prescribing, but larger studies should be done to confirm this finding.
