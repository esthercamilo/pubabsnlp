<b>Background:</b> Intestinal pathology in cystic fibrosis (CF) remains mechanistically underexplored. <b>Aim:</b> We hypothesized that differential correlation network analysis of expression data would reveal hub genes of CF-related disturbance in the large bowel. <b>Materials & methods:</b> Transcriptomes of 29 rectal tissue samples were accessed at ArrayExpress (E-GEOD-15568 by Stanke <i>et al.</i>). <b>Results:</b> We identified 279 transcript pairs differentially correlating in CF and controls, including: <i>ESRRA</i> and <i>RPL18</i> (r<sub>CF</sub> = 0.55; r<sub>controls</sub> = -0.68; p<sub>adj</sub> = 1.60 × 10<sup>-100</sup>), <i>SRP14</i> and <i>FAU</i> (r<sub>CF</sub> = -0.69; r<sub>controls</sub> = 0.48; p<sub>adj</sub> = 2.99 × 10<sup>-90</sup>), <i>SRP14</i> and <i>GDI2</i> (r<sub>CF</sub> = -0.34; r<sub>controls</sub> = 0.60; p<sub>adj</sub> = 1.05 × 10<sup>-78</sup>). The genes related to membrane protein targeting (q = 8.34 × 10<sup>-14</sup>) and one cluster was clearly linked to male infertility. <b>Conclusion:</b><i>FAU</i>, <i>SRP14</i> and <i>GDI2</i> may be involved in a compensatory protein trafficking mechanism in CF rectum, highlighting their potential therapeutic value.
