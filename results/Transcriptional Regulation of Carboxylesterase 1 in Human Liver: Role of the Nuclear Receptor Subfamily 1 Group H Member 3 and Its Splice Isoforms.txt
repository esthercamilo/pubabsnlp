Carboxylesterase 1 (<i>CES1</i>) is the predominant carboxylesterase in the human liver, involved in metabolism of both xenobiotics and endogenous substrates. Genetic or epigenetic factors that alter <i>CES1</i> activity or expression are associated with changes in drug response, lipid, and glucose homeostasis. However, the transcriptional regulation of <i>CES1</i> in the human liver remains uncertain. By applying both the random forest and Sobol's Sensitivity Indices (SSI) to analyze existing liver RNA expression microarray data (GSE9588), we identified nuclear receptor subfamily 1 group H member 3 (<i>NR1H3</i>) liver X receptor (LXR)<i>α</i> as a key factor regulating constitutive <i>CES1</i> expression. This model prediction was validated using small interfering RNA (siRNA) knockdown and CRISPR-mediated transcriptional activation of <i>NR1H3</i> in Huh7 and HepG2 cells. We found that <i>NR1H3</i>'s activation of <i>CES1</i> is splice isoform-specific, namely that increased expression of the <i>NR1H3-211</i> isoform increased <i>CES1</i> expression whereas <i>NR1H3-201</i> did not. Also, in human liver samples, expression of <i>NR1H3</i>-211 and <i>CES1</i> are correlated, whereas <i>NR1H3-201</i> and <i>CES1</i> are not. This trend also occurs during differentiation of induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) to hepatocytes, where only expression of the <i>NR1H3-211</i> isoform parallels expression of <i>CES1</i> Moreover, we found that treatment with the <i>NR1H3</i> agonist T0901317 in HepG2 cells had no effect on <i>CES1</i> expression. Overall, our results demonstrate a key role of <i>NR1H3</i> in maintaining the constitutive expression of <i>CES1</i> in the human liver. Furthermore, our results support that the effect of <i>NR1H3</i> is splice isoform-specific and appears to be ligand independent. SIGNIFICANCE STATEMENT: Despite the central role of carboxylesterase 1 (CES1) in metabolism of numerous medications, little is known about its transcriptional regulation. This study identifies nuclear receptor subfamily 1 group H member 3 as a key regulator of constitutive CES1 expression and therefore is a potential target for future studies to understand interperson variabilities in CES1 activity and drug metabolism.
