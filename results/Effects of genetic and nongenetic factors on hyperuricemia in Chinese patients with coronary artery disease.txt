<b>Aim:</b> The relationship between hyperuricemia and polymorphisms of transporter genes in coronary artery disease (CAD) patients in China remains unclear. <b>Materials & methods:</b> A total of 258 hyperuricemia patients with CAD and 242 control patients with CAD were recruited in this case-control study. Twenty-four SNPs in genes of <i>ABCG2</i>, <i>PDZK1</i>, <i>URAT1</i>, <i>OAT4</i>, <i>GLUT9</i>, <i>ABCC4</i>, <i>NPT1</i> and <i>NPT4</i> were genotyped using direct sequencing in all subjects. <b>Results:</b> The mutation of <i>ABCG2</i> rs2231142 locus increases the risk of hyperuricemia, and there is a gene dose effect in the influence of mutant heterozygotes and homozygotes. rs3825017 in <i>URAT1</i> and rs62293298 in <i>GLUT9</i> were also confirmed to be associated with hyperuricemia. <b>Conclusion:</b> Age, weight, creatinine clearance rate, diuretics and SNPs on <i>ABCG2</i>, <i>URAT1</i> and <i>GLUT9</i> were all risk factors of hyperuricemia.
