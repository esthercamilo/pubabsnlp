Vitamin D deficiency is related with susceptibility or progression of various autoimmune diseases. The aim of the study was to assess potential relations between single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) in the vitamin D receptor-coding gene (<i>VDR</i>): rs1544410 (<i>Bsm</i>I), rs2228570 (<i>Fok</i>I), rs731236 (<i>Taq</i>I), rs7975232 (<i>Apa</i>I), and disease activity in patients with axial spondyloarthritis (axSpA) undergoing anti-TNF therapy. The <i>VDR</i> rs731236 <i>CT</i> genotype was statistically more common among female patients (<i>p</i> = 0.027). An improvement of CRP equal to or higher than 50% after 3 months of anti-TNF therapy was observed for rs2228570 <i>T</i> allele (<i>p</i> = 0.002). After 6 months, CRP improvement equal to or higher than 75% was related to presence of the rs1544410 <i>AA</i> genotype (<i>p</i> = 0.027) and the rs731236 <i>CC</i> homozygotes (<i>p</i> = 0.047). Baseline BASDAI values were lower in individuals with the rs2228570 <i>TT</i> genotype (<i>p</i> = 0.036) and rs7975232 <i>C</i> allele (<i>p</i> = 0.029). After 6 months of treatment, lower BASDAI values were observed in <i>AC</i> heterozygotes (<i>p</i> = 0.005). The same <i>AC</i> genotype was more frequently detected in patients with remission (BASDAI ≤ 2) (<i>p</i> = 0.001) and in those achieving BASDAI improvement equal to or higher than 75% (<i>p</i> = 0.006). In conclusion, <i>VDR</i> SNPs were found to relate to CRP and BASDAI values at different time points of anti-TNF therapy.
