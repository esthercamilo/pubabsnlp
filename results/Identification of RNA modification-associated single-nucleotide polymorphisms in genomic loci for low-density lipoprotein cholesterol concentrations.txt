<b>Introduction:</b> Genome-wide association studies have identified approximately 1000 lipid-associated loci, but functional variants are less known. <b>Materials & methods:</b> The authors identified RNA modification-related single-nucleotide polymorphisms (RNAm-SNPs) in summary data from a genome-wide association study. By applying Mendelian randomization analysis, the authors identified gene expression levels involved in the regulation of RNAm-SNPs on low-density lipoprotein cholesterol (LDL-C) levels. <b>Results:</b> The authors identified 391 RNAm-SNPs that were significantly associated with LDL-C levels. RNAm-SNPs in <i>NPC1L1</i>, <i>LDLR</i>, <i>APOB</i>, <i>MYLIP</i>, <i>LDLRAP1</i> and <i>ABCA6</i> were identified. The RNAm-SNPs were associated with gene expression. The expression levels of 112 genes were associated with LDL-C levels, and some of them (e.g., <i>APOB</i>, <i>SMARCA4</i> and <i>SH2B3</i>) were associated with coronary artery disease. <b>Conclusion:</b> This study identified many RNAm-SNPs in LDL-C loci and elucidated the relationship among the SNPs, gene expression and LDL-C.
