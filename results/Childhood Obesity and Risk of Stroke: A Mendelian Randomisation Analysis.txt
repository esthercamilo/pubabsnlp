<b>Background:</b> The causal relationship between childhood obesity and stroke remains unclear. Our objective was to elucidate the causal relationship between childhood obesity and the risk of stroke and its subtypes by performing Mendelian randomisation (MR) analyses. <b>Methods:</b> Genetic instruments for childhood obesity were obtained from a genome-wide association study (GWAS) of 13,848 European participants. Summary level data for stroke, intracerebral haemorrhage, ischaemic stroke (IS), and its subtypes were evaluated using the MEGASTROKE GWAS dataset, which included 446,696 European adults. Inverse-variance weighting, weighted-median analysis, MR-Egger regression, MR Pleiotropy RESidual Sum and Outlier test (MR-PRESSO), and MR-Robust Adjusted Profile Score were applied in this MR analysis. The leave-one-out sensitivity test, MR-PRESSO Global test, and Cochran's Q test were conducted to confirm the accuracy and robustness of our results. <b>Results:</b> Genetic evaluations revealed that childhood obesity was associated with a higher risk of stroke (OR = 1.04, 95%CI: 1.01-1.07, <i>p</i> = 0.005) and IS (OR = 1.05, 95%CI: 1.02-1.08, <i>p</i> = 0.003), but not with intracerebral haemorrhage (ICH, OR = 0.93, 95%CI: 0.80-1.09, <i>p</i> = 0.39). In the subtype analysis, childhood obesity was also associated with large artery stroke (LAS, OR = 1.12, 95%CI: 1.02-1.22, <i>p</i> = 0.016) but not with cardioembolic stroke (OR = 1.06, 95%CI: 0.96-1.18, <i>p</i> = 0.21) and small vessel stroke (OR = 1.06, 95%CI: 0.98-1.15, <i>p</i> = 0.17). These results were stable in the sensitivity analysis and remained significant after Bonferroni correction. <b>Conclusion:</b> Our study provides evidence that childhood obesity is associated with a higher risk of stroke, IS, and LAS. The prevention of stroke, especially IS and LAS, should be promoted in populations with childhood obesity.
