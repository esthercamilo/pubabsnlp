<b>Aim:</b> To explore the pharmacogenetic differentiation across Latin American populations, using the fixation index statistics (F<sub><i>ST</i></sub>). <b>Materials & methods:</b> F<sub><i>ST</i></sub> analyses were applied to 1519 pharmacogenetic markers in the 1000 Genomes admixed American superpopulation (1KG_AMR) and an admixed Brazilian sample. <b>Results:</b> Allele-specific F<sub><i>ST</i></sub> values for the overall cohort point to little overall pharmacogenetic differentiation (average F<sub><i>ST</i></sub> = 0.017); however, moderate differentiation (F<sub><i>ST</i></sub> = 0.05-0.15) was observed for 83 markers, while large differentiation (F<sub><i>ST</i></sub> = 0.15-0.25) was restricted to three markers. Pairwise F<sub><i>ST</i></sub> analysis identified three markers with very large differentiation (F<sub><i>ST</i></sub> >0.25). <b>Conclusion:</b> The present study verifies and extends previous reports of little overall pharmacogenetic divergence across Latin America, although a number of markers display substantial differentiation.
