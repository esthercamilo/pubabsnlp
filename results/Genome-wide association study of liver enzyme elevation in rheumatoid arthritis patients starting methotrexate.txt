<b>Aim:</b> To identify novel genetic variants predisposing to elevation of Alanine aminotransferase (ALT) in rheumatoid arthritis (RA) patients after initiation of methotrexate (MTX) treatment. <b>Patients & methods:</b> We performed genome-wide association studies in 198 RA patients starting MTX. Outcomes were maximum level of ALT and ALT >1.5-times the upper level of normal within the first 6 months of treatment. <b>Results:</b><i>RAVER2</i> (rs72675408) was significantly associated with maximum level of ALT (p = 4.36 × 10<sup>-8</sup>). This variant is in linkage disequilibrium with rs72675451, which is associated with differential expression of <i>JAK1</i> and <i>RAVER2</i>. <b>Conclusion:</b> We found an association between ALT elevation and genetic variants that may regulate the expression of <i>JAK1</i> and <i>RAVER2</i>. <i>JAK1</i> encodes a janus kinase involved in the pathogenesis of RA.
