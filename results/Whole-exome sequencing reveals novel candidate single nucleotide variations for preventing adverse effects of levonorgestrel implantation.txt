<b>Aim:</b> To identify novel genes associated with adverse effects of levonorgestrel (LNG) implants based on comparative whole-exome sequencing. <b>Materials & methods:</b> A cohort comprising 104 participants, including 52 controls and 52 women with LNG-related adverse effects, was recruited. Seven cases and eight controls were selected for whole-exome sequencing. We verified 13 single nucleotide variations (SNVs) related with integrin-mediated signaling pathway and cell proliferation using the MassARRAY platform. <b>Results:</b> Finally, we screened 49 cases and 52 controls for analyses. Two SNVs (rs7255721 and rs1042522) were located in <i>ADAMTS10</i> and <i>TP53</i>, respectively, and significantly different between two groups. These two SNVs lead to changes in protein structure and physicochemical parameters. <b>Conclusion:</b> Here, we defined two pathogenic mutations related to adverse LNG effects.
