Ammonia (NH<sub>3</sub>) and hydrazine (N<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub>) present potential risks to human health, food and environmental safety. A sustainable flavonol-based probe, quercetin pentaacetate (QPA, weak blue emission 417 nm), was fabricated for dual-ratiometric fluorescent sensing and visual differentiating NH<sub>3</sub> and N<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub>. Excited state intramolecular proton transfer-on products with green (487 nm) and yellow (543 nm) emissions occurred as meeting with NH<sub>3</sub> and N<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub>, respectively, for their different nucleophilicities. Such a promising response offered a great opportunity of QPA to discriminatively detect NH<sub>3</sub> and N<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub> with large Stokes shifts (>122 nm), high sensitivity (limit of detection: 35.4 μM and 0.70 ppm for NH<sub>3</sub> solution and gas; 0.26 μM for N<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub> solution), excellent accuracy (spiked recoveries from 98.6 % to 105 %), and superior selectivity. Importantly, QPA was utilized for monitoring NH<sub>3</sub> vapor in fish spoilage procedures and detecting N<sub>2</sub>H<sub>4</sub> in water samples for food and environmental safety evaluation.
